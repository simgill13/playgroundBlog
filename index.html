4 Reasons Why Buzzfeed's "Worth It" Videos are Awesome.

Posted by Palvinder Jagait on Mar 31, 2017 9:35:00 AM
Find me on:
LinkedIn
  • Tweet

Buzzfeed is kinda awesome and kinda annoying. They put out so much content, most of it is bound to be annoying, but here and there, they are also bound to hit somethings perfect.

 

One thing they hit perfectly: the "Worth It" food videos.

 

These are series of videos where 2 guys go out and try certain types of foods at 3 different price points. For example, sushi... $3 sushi VS. $20 sushi VS. $250 sushi. It's insanely addicting and hugely popular... if you haven't seen them check out these.

 

$3 Sushi Vs. $250 Sushi

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2GZrPR5Ajrs

 

$11 Steak Vs. $306 Steak

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z5FoX3ju2J0

 

 

SO... WHY ARE THEY SO POPULAR??

 

1. They Are High-Concept

 

"High-Concept" means an idea you can understand easily within very few words. "Try Cheap, Medium and Expensive Foods" is a very easy to understand "high-concept". Most things that become VERY POPULAR are high-concept. The opposite of high-concept would be "The nutritional differences between foods from grocery stores in Southern California"... it doesn't quite have that simple ring to it... you would need to ask more questions like, "why"?

 

2. Themes

 

One of the coolest aspects to the series is the little things they do over and over in every episode. These things seem trivial, but help you build strong attachment to the series and characters over time. What are some themes?

 

1. One guy is always happy-go-lucky, the other pessimistic, the camera guys is innocent

2. The Food facts

3. "Cheers'ing" the food every time before they eat

4. The two characters eating from the same plate

5. The Camera guy always getting to eat the food before they leave

6. The Classical music when showing the prep of the most expensive place

7. The Car Ride to the places

Etc, etc, etc...

 

3. Explaining How to Make the Food

 

This is a pretty typical thing in cooking shows, channels, etc... But, the fact that they take the time to show the prep, rather than just showing the eating, helps tap into the huge additional market for people who love learning how food is made.

 

4. It's Quick

 

One thing you should always keep in mind, if you are producing content for online use... KEEP IT QUICK! Although the "Worth It" videos range in time, if you notice how often they cut, you'll see why they never lose your attention. The characters have such good personalities, that they just keep rolling on them and just cut out any part that borders on boring. The video still turns out to be 10-12 minutes long, but it just rolls along and you don't even notice.

 

So that's my analysis on why these videos are so awesome. Go watch them if you haven't!
